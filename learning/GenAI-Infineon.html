University Project

GenAI Strategy for Marketing at Infineon

This project study at TUM, conducted in collaboration with Infineon Technologies, gave me the opportunity to work on a real strategic question: how can Generative AI be implemented in a successful and scalable way within a semiconductor marketing organization?

Working together with another student, we analyzed the current role of GenAI in the department and explored where it could create the most value. We conducted interviews with marketing employees to better understand their existing workflows, their expectations toward AI, and the main obstacles preventing broader adoption. These conversations helped us move beyond general assumptions and identify concrete use cases as well as barriers to internal AI adoption. Based on our findings, we developed a roadmap for GenAI implementation in marketing. The roadmap included immediate actions to support current users, as well as longer-term recommendations for integrating AI more systematically into processes and structures.

What I found especially valuable about this project was the combination of technology, marketing, strategy, and change management. It showed me that introducing AI is not only about choosing the right tools, but also about helping people understand, trust, and apply them in their daily work, as well as adapting the whole company and its processes around it. Because the project was based on a real business challenge from Infineon, the outcome had to be practical and actionable, which made the experience especially rewarding.
