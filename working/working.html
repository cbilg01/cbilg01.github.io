Working

It’s hard to single out individual projects or activities from my work experience, since my learning has been a continuous process throughout each role. Instead, I’ve selected a few reflections on key insights and the experiences behind them.

Strategy

When corporate feels like a startup: building communication for digitalization

My most recent professional experience was the most challenging but also rewarding: the Digitalization team at Infineon had grown significantly over the past year and wanted to strengthen its communication. From day one, I was tasked with building a communication plan to increase awareness across the organization. On top of that, I was responsible for both implementation and evaluation.

Read more

I started with aligning the team’s story, key messages, and visual identity, then I created the main communication channels and started developing awareness campaigns. In short, I built a communication workflow for the department from the ground up and helped kick off its broader communication journey. The process was not always straightforward, but by the end of my experience at Infineon, I was proud of what we had achieved and how far the communication had come, and excited to see it continue with the colleague who took over my work. This is just a brief overview, as the learnings go much deeper, and I’ll happily expand on some of them in the sections below.

Marketing

Repositioning an established product

At Bosch, one of the projects I particularly enjoyed was supporting the repositioning of an established fire alarm system. The product was integrated into a new business umbrella, which required a complete shift in its narrative, target audience, and overall market positioning. The first step was to redefine the product storyline, clarifying who we were speaking to, which customer needs and pain points we were addressing, and which key benefits should be emphasized. From there, we translated this into a new positioning and communication approach that aligned with the broader strategic direction of the portfolio. I was responsible for shaping this initial narrative and supporting the rebranding process under the guidance of the Head of Marketing. Unfortunately, I did not see the process through to the end, but it was still a valuable experience in understanding how established products can be strategically reframed within a new context.

General Skills

Tables Turned

One particularly interesting experience (not directly tied to my main professional focus, but highly insightful) was supporting hiring activities. As part of my role in Corporate Communication for Digitalization, we decided to bring in a second working student to strengthen the team, and I was entrusted with leading the hiring process. Suddenly, the “tables turned”: I went from being a candidate to being responsible for evaluating candidates. My tasks included CV screening, conducting interviews, and contributing to the development and evaluation of a case study. This experience gave me a completely new perspective on recruitment and team building, and offered valuable insight into how hiring decisions are made. It was also a meaningful first exposure to a responsibility I expect to take on more frequently in future roles.

Events

Events & I

My experience with events started early in my career at Sweet Communication, with seasonal agency events where clients presented new collections. On a more corporate level, I also supported the organization of Bosch’s annual Sales Meeting in Valencia, a 100-person event where product managers and sales teams align on product and marketing topics. Here, I contributed to the agenda, coordination with product managers, content flow, and on-site organization to ensure everything ran smoothly. At Infineon, I applied this experience to internal communication events, including an annual showcase of the digitalization team’s work, featuring speaker notes, solution presentations, and a panel discussion. In addition, I supported the organization of the Rising Star Alumni Conference in Dresden, a 35-person event focused on networking and talent development within the company. Across all experiences, one thing became clear: events are complex and often unpredictable, but they are incredibly rewarding, especially when everything comes together on the day.
